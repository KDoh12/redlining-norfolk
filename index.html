1940 Redlining in Norfolk: Now vs Then

University of Kentucky New Maps Plus

Redlining is a discriminatory practice of refusing services to a specif area or neighborhood based on certain characteristics. Click on the link below the map or click here for a full-screen version of my map.
The colored areas on the map above are the actual boundaries created by the Home Owner Loan Corporation (HOLC) during the 1930s and 40s. They are colored based on the "grade" they were assigned by the HOLC which is A, B, C, and D grades with "A" being deemed the "best" areas for investment, and "D" being areas that investors should avoid unless absolutely necessary. If you click on the areas you will see some economic, home-ownership, and demographic data from both the 1940s and from 2019. The idea is to get a sense of what those areas were like back in 1940 as well has how they are today and examine any similarities or differences.
